Blue River, Oregon · McKenzie River · 6-day immersion

Welcome to the 3rd Annual Breath of Life: Breathwork and IFS Retreat in Oregon!

We're so grateful to invite you to our Breath of Life Retreat! A 6-day, 5-night immersive experience set in the breathtaking natural beauty of Blue River, Oregon, along the majestic McKenzie River. Why come

7 Reasons to join this journey.

6-Day Retreat in McKenzie River, Oregon

Immerse yourself in the serene beauty of McKenzie River, Oregon, where nature's splendor invites deep reflection and renewal. This peaceful and powerful landscape offers the perfect setting for self-discovery and healing. During our retreat, you'll connect with a community of like-minded individuals, share in transformative experiences, and tap into the wisdom of the natural world. Through nature hikes, natural cold plunges, breathwork and internal work, you will reconnect with yourself and uncover a deeper sense of purpose and peace.

A Journey of Insight and Inner Transformation

This retreat is designed to facilitate profound inner transformation and self-discovery. Our program offers a unique blend of guided experiential practices, including morning and evening circles, somatic embodiment practices such as breathwork, movement, and nature-based activities. You'll engage in activities that promote deep connection, from invigorating hikes along the McKenzie River and natural cold plunges to community sharing and reflective internal work. Each day, you'll be immersed in nature and supported in uncovering deeper insights, fostering lasting change and personal growth.

Retreat Venue

Tucked neatly along the bank of the McKenzie River rests a row of historic cottages and suites with their own unique charm and personalities. The McKenzie River Cottages are perched over the river with brisk water flowing from Clear Lake. Each unit is connected to the wrap around patio overlooking the running river where we will do our breathwork, meditation, yoga and relaxation.

Intimate Group Setting

Our retreat creates space for meaningful connection, personal growth, and genuine community. In a welcoming and supportive environment, participants are encouraged to engage authentically, share openly, and learn from one another. Through shared experiences, we cultivate a sense of belonging, mutual support, and deeper connection with ourselves and those around us.

4 Facilitators

Together, Nick, Paulina, Aundi and Amanda bring a deeply integrative, heart-centered approach to healing and self-discovery. Blending breathwork, Internal Family Systems, yoga, mindfulness, somatic practices, and intentional community, their facilitation supports nervous-system regulation, emotional healing, and embodied self-leadership. Each facilitator contributes a unique lens shaped by years of clinical training, lived experience, and retreat leadership, creating a grounded and compassionate container where participants feel safe to explore, release, and reconnect with their authentic selves. United by a shared commitment to trauma-informed care, presence, and meaningful transformation, they guide experiences that invite clarity, resilience, and lasting inner change.

The practice

Conscious Connected Breathwork.

Conscious Connected Breathwork will be a cornerstone of our retreat. This technique provides a safe and effective way to connect with your inner body wisdom, helping to activate and enhance the healing process. By soothing the rational mind and allowing emotions to emerge, this practice encourages deep emotional release and transformation. The breathwork process shifts your physiological state, enabling exploration of altered states of consciousness.

Combining IFS therapy with breathwork creates a powerful synergy for self-exploration and trauma recovery. Breathwork enables individuals to connect more deeply with their inner parts, facilitating access to Self-energy and releasing emotional burdens. Meanwhile, IFS therapy addresses and dissolves internal barriers and protective mechanisms, allowing individuals to surrender fully and tune into their inner guidance and bodily wisdom.

This 6-day retreat offers a transformative inner journey, blending the powerful practice of Conscious Connected Breathwork with the profound depth of IFS techniques.

You'll have the opportunity to create space for your inner experiences and address them with trusted guidance. It will feel like coming home to yourself—a spacious and welcoming environment for all aspects that need attention.

The guided group work will help you embody a more compassionate presence in your therapeutic practice, equipping you with advanced tools to elevate your work to the next level.

The team

Meet the facilitators.

Nick Terry TNT

Breathwork + recovery

Nick Terry TNT is a breathwork and retreat facilitator with over a decade of experience in wellness and recovery. He specializes in conscious connected breathwork to support emotional release, nervous-system regulation, and reconnection to self-trust and purpose.

He co-founded Breath of Life Wellness, co-owns Honu House Hawaii, and is the best-selling author of Breath of Life: Finding Long-Term Recovery with Breathwork and Stepwork. Nick's facilitation blends breathwork, cold immersion, mindfulness, and community to create trauma-informed, heart-centered spaces where people can release, reconnect, and remember who they are.

Amanda Dykann

Yoga + spiritual coach

Amanda Dykann is a yoga teacher, retreat leader, and spiritual coach with nearly 20 years of experience. For over 15 years, she has guided luxury yoga retreats around the world, blending movement, mindfulness, and holistic healing in breathtaking locations.

Trained in Bikram, Vinyasa, and Yin yoga, and holding a master's degree in Spiritual Psychology from the University of Santa Monica, Amanda creates deeply transformative experiences rooted in both yoga and spiritual growth.

Paundi

Internal Family Systems

Paulina and Aundi, PAUNDI, are honored to share the insight and lived experience they have gained through their education and advanced training in Internal Family Systems (IFS). Both are currently pursuing their doctorate degrees in psychology and are Level 3 IFS trained.

Becoming IFS trained in the same year marked a meaningful alignment and the beginning of a shared path in this work. What began as parallel journeys naturally evolved into a shared mission to bring IFS into the world in a way that feels accessible, embodied, relatable, and fun.
